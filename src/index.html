CSCAN

CSCAN is an offline-first, AI-powered scanning system built to detect and flag suspected CSAM in both images and videos - before it reaches your users, your infrastructure, or your compliance boundary.

Designed for easy integration into any upload or ingestion pipeline, CSCAN supports transactional or batch processing, leverages parallel execution for speed, and provides reliable, resumable analysis at scale - all with minimal latency and zero cloud dependencies.

Real-time certificate transparency monitoring

Sift

Sift monitors over 24 certificate transparency log providers in real-time, creating keyword-based alerts to surface abuse-related domains before they go live. Every SSL certificate issued leaves a public record - Sift watches all of them.

An optional DGA module identifies algorithmically generated domains - a common evasion technique used by malicious actors. Created from the ground up using a proprietary neural network trained on millions of known DGA domains.

Multi-layer geolocation analysis

Orion

Orion is an automated OSINT tool designed for investigators. Submit a photo or video and Orion extracts embedded metadata - GPS coordinates, device fingerprints, timestamps - and cross-references detected visual landmarks to pinpoint a location.

Built to help child protection investigators identify where an image was taken and narrow down who may be in danger. All analysis runs locally with no data leaving your environment.

Darknet and Clearnet Domain Monitoring

Deep Crawl

Deep Crawl continuously scans darknet (TOR) and clearnet websites for indicators of CSAM and other abuse-related content distribution. By mapping website hosting patterns, coding heuristics, and distribution methods, Deep Crawl identifies and flags the domain, collecting metadata to help investigators understand how content is being shared and who may be behind it over periods of time.

By understanding the infrastructure and methods used to distribute abuse-related content, platforms can proactively block harmful domains before they reach users, and investigators can identify perpetrators and victims more quickly.
